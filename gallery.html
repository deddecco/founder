CNET President George W. Bush gave Cerf the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the country's highest honor, in 2005. His citation for the Medal, explaining why he was awarded it, taken from the White House's website, reads,

"More than 30 years ago, Vinton Cerf and Robert Kahn designed the architecture and communication protocol that gave rise to the modern Internet. The innovative work of these two pioneers laid the foundation for a global transformation of communication, commerce, and entertainment. The United States honors Vinton Cerf and Robert Kahn for their outstanding contributions to science and for improving the lives of all Americans." Cerf is with Kahn and the President in the picture.

Geeks for Geeks A comparison between the TCP/IP and OSI models. OSI has 7 layers, while TCP/IP has only 4. Several layers in OSI-- another protocol for managing the flow of information across networks-- collapse into single layers in the TCP/IP scheme. The modern internet is built around TCP/IP rather than OSI. However, both models allow computers to communicate with each other and exchange information between themselves.
Network World For having come up with TCP/IP, Cerf, together with Bob Kahn, won the Turing Award (given each year by the ACM, considered roughly the "Nobel in Computer Science") in 2004. The citation for his 2004 prize reads as follows:"With Robert E. Kahn, for pioneering work on internetworking, including the design and implementation of the Internet's basic communications protocols, TCP/IP, and for inspired leadership in networking."
